How to use:


What is a foot traffic heatmap?

A foot traffic heatmap is a way to visualize how people are walking through a space. The brighter the color, the greater the amount of time that people have been in that space. Check out the example below:


Features

  • Use the live feed from any number of cameras already connected to your computer
  • Utilizes open-source machine-leraning models to detect people using the cameras
  • Combine the outputs from multiple cameras into one heatmap
  • Completely free and open source!


Why would you want a foot traffic heatmap?

Having foot traffic data about your space is actionable data that allows you to make informed decisions about how to change the layout of your space. Whether you are an architect prototyping the layout of a new project or a retail store owner trying to optimize where certain products are placed to maximize time spent in your store, this information is of use to you. And our web app makes it dead simple.I


Instructions

  1. Connect any number of cameras to your computer
  2. Mute any cameras you don't want to use (if you have a webcam, it will be displayed so you may want to mute it)
  3. Heatmaps are best-made from a top-down view; however, we know that isn't always possible to achieve. You can fix that using the "Calibrate" button under each camera feed; learn more here .
